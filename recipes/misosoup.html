Miso Soup

Description

Miso soup is a traditional Japanese soup made with dashi (soup stock made with kombu or bonito flakes), miso paste and various ingredients depending on regional and seasonal recipes.

While it is usually served together with a salad as part of an appetizer in the US, miso soup comes with rice as part of a main meal in Japan.

Ingredients

  • One large onion
  • Four cups of water
  • One tablespoon of dashi granules
  • Three-Four tablespoons of awase miso paste
  • Ichimi togarashi (optional)

Steps

  1. Put the sliced onions, water and dashi granule in a pot and bring to a boil. Lower the heat, cover and cook for 10-12 minutes, until the onions are tender.
  2. Place the miso paste in a small strainer and lower the strainer into the pot, until the liquid covers the miso paste. Stir the miso paste with chopsticks until it has fully dissolved.
  3. Turn the heat off and serve. Sprinkle a little ichimi for heat. Enjoy!
